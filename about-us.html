Second
generation Baltic Amber maker

We take pride in being second-generation amber makers hailing from the charming Lithuanian coastal town of Nida, nestled by the Curonian Lagoon. Our roots run deep in this scenic locale.

Meet Algimantas, our lead amber maker, whose expertise extends back to 2006, while his family members started working with the Baltic gem back in 1970. With a passion for sustainable amber processing, he has successfully showcased our production technology at various amber and gem expos across Europe and the US. Algimantas embodies our commitment to not just creating exquisite amber pieces but doing so in a way that respects the environment and meets the highest standards of production ethics.

Algimantas, devoted sixteen years to the amber business, seeking innovative ways to utilize amber remnants intelligently. The distinctive Amberdam bracelets emerge as a testament to his commitment to sustainable fashion and eco-conscious practices. These products are meticulously crafted from natural Lithuanian amber fragments, utilizing a patented pressing technology prevalent in the amber industry.

The process involves transforming secondary raw materials, such as small amber pieaces, into refined amber strips. The amber particles undergo a precise compression technique, resulting in a versatile material that is later cut, shaped, polished, and formed into stylish artwork. What sets Amberdam apart is its commitment to maintaining the natural color of naturally mechanically treated amber, without the use of any chemical additives.

Algimantas actively oversees the entire technological process in his workshop in Nida, Lithuania. His dedication extends to ensuring that every stage of the bracelet creation, all twelve of them, adheres to eco-friendly practices. This cyclical approach, where leftovers are repurposed, aligns with Algimantas's vision of using natural resources rationally and minimizing waste.

The appeal of Amberdam extends beyond fashion; it represents a shift towards sustainable living. In Algimantas's view, sustainable fashion, particularly recycled material accessories, is gaining popularity in Europe. Cities like Berlin, Amsterdam, Stockholm showcase boutiques featuring accessories crafted from recycled materials, and many more are adopting this philosophy.

The minimalist design of Amberdam bracelets makes them versatile, complementing various outfits and appealing to a broad audience, irrespective of age or gender. Algimantas's ambition goes beyond creating stylish accessories; he envisions a broader cultural shift towards conscious consumption and sustainable fashion practices.

To further promote this ethos, Amberdam has collaborated with "Audimas" to integrate black bracelets into Lithuania's Olympic team apparel. Beyond being fashionable accessories, these bracelets symbolize national support, strengthen the sense of national identity, and foster a community spirit.

Amidst the rising popularity of Amberdam, Algimantas remains passionate about the idea that fashion is not merely an object but a way of life. His endeavor to make recycled material accessories a fashionable phenomenon is not just a business pursuit; it's a movement towards a more sustainable and eco-conscious future.
